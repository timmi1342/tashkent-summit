The Trip

One weekend. Every reason you booked it.

No 80km death-march from the city. We drive you and the gear to base, then it's a scenic ride, a waterfall hike, a summit, and an alpine swim — bookended by yoga, a fire, and a chef who cooks shashlyk and a proper vegan spread on the mountain.

🚲 Scenic ride

A 15–25km loop on real or e-bikes. The ride is the highlight, not the punishment.

⛰️ 2,000m summit

Kumbel Peak on fresh Day-2 legs. Hike it or take the cable car — same summit.

🏊 Alpine lake

Swim, kayak, and SUP on Charvak reservoir before the ride home.

🔥 Chef + yoga

Shashlyk and a full vegan menu, plus recovery yoga and breathwork.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

Do I need to be super fit?

No. The Explorer tier uses an e-bike and the cable car, so anyone reasonably active can do it. Standard suits regular gym-goers and hikers. Athlete is for experienced mountain hikers.

Is it safe? No phone signal up there.

Every trip has a certified guide with wilderness first-aid, a satellite communicator, accident insurance, and a support van for evacuation. You'll sign a liability waiver at booking.

I'm vegan / vegetarian.

Fully covered — grilled vegetable and mushroom skewers, vegan plov, lentil stews, grilled corn, stuffed peppers and more. Tell us your dietary needs when you book.

What's included?

Transfers from Tashkent, bikes, all camping gear, all meals, guide and instructor, lake activities, and insurance. You bring trail shoes, water shoes, layers, and swimwear.

When do trips run?

Weekends, May through September. Spots are limited to 8–12 people per trip.
